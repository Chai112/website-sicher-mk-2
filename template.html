Overview

Process Safety Management (PSM) is a vital tool used in the safety field to provide a systematic approach to preventing the catastrophic release of highly hazardous substances. PSM is a standard set forth by the Industrial Estate Authority of Thailand (IEAT), which the United States of America has adopted (USA)’s Occupational Safety & Health Administration (OSHA) since 2017 to prevent unwanted releases of highly hazardous substances into the environment that could expose employees and other to severe hazards.
Sicher is a leading company in Process Safety Management. Our Thai, German, Chinese, and Malay exert teams provide PSM Training, Consulting, or Auditing services from small manufacturing facilities to large refineries, chemical plants, and power plants for more than 100 companies with our excellent proven records.

PSM Training

Sicher instructors train the Process engineers, maintenances, and EHS team towards increased efficiency and effectiveness in process safety management. The PSM training course is a combination of instructions and team-based exercises and teaches what is at the heart of process safety management.

PSM Consulting

We provide PSM Consultancy services, delivering tangible benefits to clients across the industry. Our core services are Process Hazard Analysis – HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Analysis), Operating Procedure development, Mechanical Integrity (MI), Management of Change (MOC), and others. We carried out numerous assignments with companies ranging from small to large chemical petroleum complexes. We pride ourselves in working *with* our clients, using international consultant teams that enhance the capability of the client's own staff.

PSM Auditing

Industrial Estate Authority of Thailand (IEAT) requires PSM compliance audits every three years. A PSM compliance audit helps companies determine whether they are doing what is required for compliance with PSM laws. Sicher has been qualified and registered officially: License number 09. Our experienced PSM auditors can support companies to determine if they are following their PSM policies and protocols.
